9 · Real estate — commercial & residential REITs

Real estate is the world’s largest asset class (~$330T). REITs are rate-sensitive (duration + leverage) and react to fiscal policy. Commercial RE (office, retail, data-center) has higher rate sensitivity than residential (apartments, SFR, homebuilders).

10 · Bitcoin & crypto — the liquidity amplifier

Bitcoin is a ~$2T asset with institutional adoption (ETFs since Jan 2024). It behaves as a “pure liquidity sponge” — amplifying risk-on/risk-off moves with ~2–3x beta to equities in drawdowns. The dollar dial is its biggest driver.

Methodology & caveats. Scenario simulation, not a forecast or investment advice. Ten dials — severity (the equity-bust shape), inflation (the CPI regime), Fed (the policy-rate path), GDP (the real-economy outcome), tariffs (the US–China trade regime), geopolitics (stable / tension / conflict), the Japanese bond market (anchored / BoJ normalization / JGB crisis), fiscal policy (austerity / neutral / stimulus), and the US dollar (weak / neutral / strong). Paths use keyframed regime assumptions with monotone-cubic interpolation over 60 months. The S&P 500 is built bottom-up from an AI complex (~45% of index cap) and the ~55% ex-AI market; the 10-year yield is a formula of the Fed rate, CPI and unemployment plus JGB, fiscal and USD term-premium shocks; bond total returns are derived from that yield and modelled credit spreads via duration; real series deflate nominal by the modelled CPI. New in v7: Commercial and residential REITs track the real-estate equity sector (so they crash and recover like stocks) plus a modest extra rate-duration sensitivity (~1.5y commercial, ~0.8y residential) and fiscal/USD tilts. Bitcoin has ~1.6x equity beta plus fiscal and USD multipliers. The fiscal dial adds CPI, equity, sector, commodity, REIT and BTC tilts; stimulus pushes the long end higher (crowding-out). The USD dial adds a “wrecking ball” effect: strong dollar crushes commodities, EM-sensitive sectors and Bitcoin; weak dollar boosts them. 12 historical-analog presets include a 2022 Rate Shock case (dials kept faithful to actual 2022) for validation. Start conditions (May 2026): S&P 500 ≈7,473; Fed funds 3.50–3.75%; 10Y ≈4.60%; CPI ≈3.0%; WTI ≈$98; DXY ≈104; BTC ≈$108K; ~$1.4T AI-linked debt; effective US tariffs on China ≈31%.
